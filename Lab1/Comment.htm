By playing with the parameters of the Gaussian filter you may have noted the apparition of some artefacts while using a big standard deviation and a small radius. This is normal. For these cases you do not apply a Gaussian filter, but only the central part of a Gaussian.

Defining a radius 3 times bigger than standard deviation is sufficient, because the significance of Gaussian values farer from the centre of the Gaussian are close enough from 0 to have close to no influence. You may verify that by keeping the same standard deviation and increasing the radius for the Gaussian filter.
